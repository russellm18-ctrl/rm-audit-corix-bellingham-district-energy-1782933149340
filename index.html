// Findings

  • THE STRUCTURAL METADATA COLLAPSE: Your portfolio images—the Seattle Spheres enclosure, Overlake FutureCare East Tower, Federal Center South—are carrying zero structured data weight. No JSON-LD schema ties those project images to geographic identifiers, project types, or service categories, and absent alt-text means search engines cannot parse them as anything other than blank pixels. You are bleeding the query "mechanical contractor healthcare Seattle" directly to competitors whose project photography is tagged, geolocated, and schema-wrapped as LocalBusiness and ConstructionProject entities. Every untagged image in your Overlake FutureCare portfolio is an indexed dead end.
  • THE HIDDEN TRUST DEFICIT: Your target signatory—a healthcare or institutional facilities director evaluating you for a multi-million dollar systems contract—lands on written claims like "we respect your time and budget" with zero photographic evidence of actual installed mechanical systems, operational environments, or documented project outcomes. The Overlake East Tower entry is a prime example: the written brief is clinically vague, and there is no visual proof of completed mechanical infrastructure, phased construction sequencing, or code-compliance documentation that a healthcare procurement officer demands before shortlisting. Words without visual corroboration read as marketing copy, not qualifications.
  • THE COMPETITIVE EXPOSURE: Hard-bid mechanical contractors targeting the same Pacific Northwest healthcare and institutional market—firms like Lease Crutcher Lewis's trade partners or McKinstry—are deploying structured project photography that includes annotated BIM-to-build comparisons, commissioning documentation imagery, and facility services team photography tied to specific service agreement deliverables. Your "Facility Services" and "Building Automation" pages carry no supporting visual assets whatsoever, meaning any facilities manager comparing vendor sites will assign credibility to the firm that visually documents its operational depth—and that is not currently you, costing you service agreement renewals and preconstruction invitations.
  • THE PHOTO-FIRST UPGRADE: Commission a structured asset capture across your four active portfolio categories—Preconstruction, Build, Facility Services, and Building Automation—shooting installed systems, active service agreement sites, and team-in-environment documentation at locations like Federal Center South, which you explicitly still maintain. Every image file must exit the shoot pre-loaded with EXIF location data, a standardized naming taxonomy tied to service line and geography, and deployed with project-specific JSON-LD schema and descriptive alt-text referencing the system type, building use, and Washington State location. This converts your photography from a passive design element into a crawlable, indexable project credential library that works as a persistent qualification asset between every RFP cycle.
